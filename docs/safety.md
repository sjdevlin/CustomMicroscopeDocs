# Safety

## Scope

Minimum safety controls for operation, maintenance, and troubleshooting of the microscope.

## Hazard register

| Hazard | Risk | Primary Controls | PPE | Residual Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Optical radiation | Eye injury | Enclosures, beam blocks, interlocks | Eye protection as required |  |
| Electrical hazards | Shock/fire | Fusing, grounding, insulated wiring | Electrical PPE for service work |  |
| Mechanical pinch/crush points | Injury | Guards, travel limits, safe clearances | Gloves as appropriate |  |
| Chemical/sample hazards | Exposure | Containment, handling SOPs, disposal protocol | Lab coat/gloves/eye protection |  |

## Mandatory controls

- Interlocks must be functional before operation.
- Emergency stop must be accessible and tested.
- Power isolation point must be labeled.
- Only trained users may operate without supervision.

## Pre-operation safety checklist

- [ ] Interlocks verified
- [ ] Emergency stop verified
- [ ] No exposed live conductors
- [ ] Appropriate PPE available and used
- [ ] Sample hazard compatibility confirmed

## Emergency response

1. Trigger emergency stop or isolate power.
2. Secure sample and illumination source.
3. Evacuate/contain area if required by lab policy.
4. Report incident per institutional process.
5. Log event in [Maintenance & Change Log](maintenance-change-log.md).
